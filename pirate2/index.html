Platform Basis

Pirate politics was born in 2006 with the launch of the Pirate Party of Sweden. The core tenets of the pirate ideology focused on copyright reform, open government and freedom of information. This would eventually be expanded to include digital rights and privacy. The Pirate Party inspired many. Pirate parties around the world were formed in the months and years that followed. There was no official connection between these pirate parties located around the globe, rather they were bound together by a common set of shared values - the pirate politics ideology.

In 2008, an Australian Pirate Party was formed. At the height of the global pirate movement the Australian party enjoyed some success. But the party executive allowed it to fall into a state of disrepute. The party lost support and members, forcing it to file for deregistration in April 28, 2021.

The pirate2 concept was born out of growing frustration at the lost momentum of the global pirate movement, particularly that of the Australian party. Pirate Party Australia has suffered from a lack of enthusiasm from its members and is evident in its tired and outdated policies. We believe there is room for a new generation of pirate politics in Australia. pirate2 wants to move Australia forward by setting a new standard of next-generation pirate politics. We want to build a platform based on an Australian Republic. We must drop all official recognition of the Commonwealth and no longer delay the inevitable path to an Australian Republic.

pirate2 believes in fair, transparent and peaceful government. While we are adament that democracy is the best model of governance, we respect that it is not the only successful model. Irrespective of the model of governance adopted, the principles of fairness, transparency and peace must always prevail. Traditional political models are broken. The concept of democracy is not lost, rather the outdated party structures and mechanisms that drive their function are failing to deliver citizens true democratic fairness and government transparency. pirate2 believes this must change and can only be delivered by embrace of reformed party politics which will deliver policies relevant to today's society, not a post-war society of a past generation.

pirate2 has been established to develop the pirate politics platform of the future, for an Australian Republic. In 2017, we saw a brief moment of promise with the launch of an alternative Australian pirate party, called Democratic Pirates Australia. The alternative platform we had developed showed real promise. pirate2 shares much heritage of Democratic Pirates. We have listened, observed and learned from past mistakes and are confident we can take a reformed approach and build a viable alternative pirate party platform for Australia. We can reinforce existing democratic values by reforming and adapting them to form a next-generation political platform which embraces technology and its values to deliver genuine fairness and transparency.

Constitional and Legislative Reform

pirate2 believes that if Australia is to transition from a member of the Commonwealth and become an independent republic nation, the current Constitution would need such great reform that it would be irresponsible to attempt to adapt the current Constitution.
We propose a complete dumping on the current outdated Constitution and an Australian republic adopt a new Constitution which is written fit-for-purpose of an Australian republic.

There are many current specifics of the current Constitution that are not only outdated and irrelevant to a modern, mature republic, but place immediate impediments on Australias ability to mature as a nation.
  • Protections for freedom of speech
  • Protections for freedom of the press
  • Restrictions of freedom of movement
  • Restrictions on State movement
  • Implement zoning system within States
  • Catagorizing of citizen social status
  • Implement social credit system
  • Implement a Universal Basic Income
  • Develop a economic transition mechanism to adapt economic structures with minimal interruption
  • Reform of natiional economic structures
  • Embrace of digital currencies with plan to phase out traditional monetary practices

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1. Why the name "pirate2"?
A. pirate2 is the development codename for the proposed platform whilst it is in development.
---
Q2. What does pirate2 advocate?
A. We advocate for the Republic of Australia to adopt a next-generation political platform, for constitutional rewrite and legislative reform to protect citizen's rights.
---
Q3. Which side of the political spectrum do you promote?
A. pirate2 does not aim to promote any side of the current political spectrum. Instead, we promote the concept of reinforcing existing values while adapting to a next-generation political platform.
---
Q4. Is pirate2 associated with the Liberal or Labor party?
A. pirate2 is not associated with any political party.
---
Q5. What are the problems with the Liberal Party?
A. pirate2 believes the party's increasing embrace of reduced liberalism, restrictions of freedoms and erosion of the free press are contrary to the party's values.
---
Q6. What are the problems with the Labor Party?
A. pirate2 believes the party suffers from lack of political relevance and continues to fail to develop policy which achieves a fair balance between liberal idealogy and responsible fiscal management.
---
Q7. What are the problems with The Greens?
A. pirate2 believes the party suffers from being constrained to its own political bubble which effectively limits its ability to maintain any political relevance through a broadening in policy scope.
---
Q8. Does pirate2 support Pirate Party Australia?
A. No.
---
Q9. Does pirate2 plan to register as a political party with the Australian Electoral Commission?
A. pirate2 has no plans to register as a political party. Our primary focus is developing a next-generation political platform.
---
Q10. Does pirate2 have international representation?
A. No. Our immediate focus is to develop a next-generation political platform for the Republic of Australia.
